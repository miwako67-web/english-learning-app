🎙 The Meaning of Life — 1-minute speaking script
0:00 / 1:00

I often find myself wondering, "What am I here for?" It's one of those questions that keeps creeping back, no matter how busy I get.


Intellectually, I have come to terms with the idea that life may have no inherent meaning. But even knowing that, I still feel a kind of existential ache — a heaviness that's hard to shake.


What gets me is this loop: if life has no meaning, then how we live shouldn't matter either — and yet, somehow, it still does. That contradiction is exhausting.


One thing that helps me is stepping back and thinking of all this as a kind of simulation. If none of it is truly "real," then maybe the suffering isn't either. It's a strange comfort, but it works for me.


I guess what I'm learning is that sitting with the uncertainty — rather than trying to solve it — might be the whole point. Not resolution, but presence.
